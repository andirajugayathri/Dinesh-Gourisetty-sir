Political Journey

Breaking Barriers in Australian Politics

Dinesh Gourisetty
March 17, 2025 · 10 min read
Cultures Community

Dinesh Gourisetty's political journey is a testament to perseverance, representing a significant milestone for multicultural representation in Australian politics.

Inspiration and Early Involvement

Inspired by former Prime Minister John Howard's policies that enabled immigrant success, Gourisetty began his political journey around 2006.

Rather than passive support, he actively volunteered for the Liberal Party, dedicating six years to grassroots campaigning before formal party membership.

Electoral Campaigns and Challenges

Gourisetty's political trajectory included several notable campaigns:

  • 2012 Wyndham City Council elections
  • 2014 Victorian state election (Tarneit seat)
  • 2018 Victorian Upper House ticket for Western Metropolitan Region

Despite not winning these elections, he gained something arguably more valuable – a reputation as a tenacious organizer and an emerging voice for multicultural communities.

Facing Systemic Challenges

His political journey was marked by significant adversity. Campaigning in diverse suburbs, Gourisetty faced racist taunts, physical intimidation, and systematic exclusion.

Colleagues recounted instances of volunteers being targeted, including threatening phone calls and even physical harassment like stones being thrown.

Breaking Internal Party Barriers

In 2020, Gourisetty was elected Chair of the Liberal Party's Western Metropolitan Regional Conference, becoming the highest-ranking party official in Melbourne's west. His most audacious move came in 2021 when he contested the Victorian Liberal Party's state presidency – becoming the first Indian-Australian to seriously challenge for the top internal party position.

A New Model of Political Engagement

Gourisetty's approach to politics transcends traditional boundaries. He represents a new model of political engagement – one that prioritizes community service, cultural understanding, and persistent advocacy over personal political gains.

His remarkable resilience in the face of systemic challenges has not only opened doors for future multicultural political leaders but has also reshaped conversations about representation and inclusivity in Australian politics.
